Gumbo

1. Make the roux. in a large pot, combine flour and oil and cook, stirring constantly on medium low heat. You have to be careful to stir it constantly, on medium low heat, so that you don’t burn it. It’s easy, but takes patience. The darker the roux, the richer the flavor!

2. Chop the veggies. When you're ready to make the GUmbo, start by chopping celery, onions, bell pepper, parsley, and your choice of any other vegetables you would like to add.

3. Brown the sausage. Add the sausage in a single layer on a hot (preferabley large) skillet and once one side is browned make sure to flip them over so they are evenly browned.

4. Add to a large pot. Add chicken broth, veggies, and roux to the pot and stir then stir in the cajun seasoning, to your taste.

5. Add meat. Add chicken, sausage, and shrimp or other meats to your taste. Add more seasonings if you would like and add more chicken bothe until you reach your desired flavor.

6. Serve Now serve your gumbo over hot rice and enjoy!

Hushpuppy

1. Preheat oil. Preheat 2 inches of oil in a large skillet to about 365 degrees.

2. Mix the dough. In a miing bowl add the flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder, salt, garlic powder, onion, powder, paprika, and pepper. Add the egg and buttermilk and stir until combined. then stir in cubed unsalted butter.

3. Cook in oil Drop the batter by rounded teaspoonfuls in the hot oil. let it them cook until they are golder brown for 2-3 minures. and then serve with tarter or garlic sauce if you want.

Collard Greens

1. Prepare the greens. Start by pulling and tearing the greens away from their stems. Take a hand full of greens, roll them up and then cut the rolls into small pieces.

2. Prepare the ham hock. Rinse the hamhok and fully submerege it in a large pot of water then cover with a lid. Cook it over medium high heat for about 3 minutes until it is near tender.

4. Add the greens. Once the ham hock is almost tender, add the greens and 4-5 more cups of water.

5. Add the rest of the ingredients. Cook all of the ingredients together for at least 2 hoours or until it's completely tender. Most of the water should be evaporated and serve!
